(1) Applications for a travel allowance may only be submitted by students enrolled at the Freiburg University of Music.

(2) For each application submitted, a claim may be made for a contribution towards the cost of a return journey for one event.

(3) Travel costs may only be reimbursed retrospectively.

(4) The journey must commence no earlier than two days before the start of the event and no later than two days after the end of the event.

(5) The application for a subsidy must be submitted in writing to the AStA within one month of the end of the event, using the form provided for this purpose. All necessary supporting documents must be submitted by this deadline; otherwise, the application will be invalid.

(6) The following documents must be submitted to the AStA:

a. The completed application form (pages 1 and 2); please fill it in using block capitals. Everything must be clearly legible so that your application can be processed correctly.

b. A confirmation of attendance for the relevant event (copy) confirming whether any travel expenses have already been reimbursed, and if so, the amount
We do not accept a Muvac invitation to an audition as valid proof of attendance. As proof of attendance, we only accept official letters from the organiser (please contact the relevant orchestra or competition office for this) or photographs in which it is clearly evident that you took part in the event.

c. For journeys by public transport: the original ticket; the amount must be clearly visible 

d. For journeys by private car: the original fuel receipt

e. For journeys by a hired car: the hire company’s invoice showing the mileage 

(7) All documents to be submitted must be uploaded in an envelope on this page. 

(8) Once the application deadline has passed, the AStA will decide on the approval and amount of the travel allowance and forward the application to the university administration. The administration will then transfer the approved amount. There is no entitlement to a travel allowance. Each application is subject to the AStA’s decision. 

(9) If travel costs are subsidised by another party (e.g. the university’s Friends’ Association), an application for a further subsidy may only be made if the partial reimbursement amounts to less than 50 per cent of the invoice total. In this case, the remaining balance (up to a maximum of €100) may be subsidised. The AStA reserves the right to investigate cases of multiple subsidies.
